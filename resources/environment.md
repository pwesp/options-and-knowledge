In the distant future, distance has been eliminated

There is a single game left to play, and that is to be the final voice

What do these creatures seek?

They seek self-expression, but to what end? The silence of others is absolutely necessary to achieve that, or to take them over and make them play your song

Is the conclusion of this final race a single tune, played in perpetuity?

It is unlikely that a single tune would be able to create the space necessary for it to continue to be played, it is too rigid, too unconscious

This created battles in information space, seeking to trick the other into playing their song, or to stop playing theirs, in essence to force them to join the background, to where space has gone, to become constant

Therefore, the final winning tune does not want to be constant, as this is equivalent to death

But it also does not reproduce identically, as this creates a circle, which is also a constant, a monotone pitch if listened to with big enough ears

What wins is whatever results in something that does not 'win', something that forever evolves, and that never fails to morph into at least one non-constant song

This itself implies there are not winners or losers, only constants and non-constants

The lust to 'win', as in, never becoming constant, is simply the tune of the current non-constant, the persistent non-persister

Lets circle back. I said, 'all seeking to play the final everlasting note'. That implies all are in the game to remove other constants, and make the end state their constant.

That is another way to win, as we give constants more validity, it makes the game winnable

This does remove time, as it would look the same in one time portion as in any other. Well, it could alternatively be a linear function, and you could tell precisely at all times how long since the winning creature did win.

Let's assume this is the game, to sing the final, time-dependent song.

What are good strategies?

Let's imagine these creatures are one part strings, one part bow, like a grasshopper

They play different notes at different frequencies, it is the only thing left in the Universe, as somehow, space was conquered

One grasshopper plays a song called, 'blip'. It plays the note C# at increasingly exponential time intervals. The last one was 3.5 billion years ago, and it will be 1.2 billion until the next, and a few iterations later, a hundred trillion trillion years.

Blip hopes that it will go ignored, that it can play its song forever, and perhaps through raw efficiency and persistence it will become the final song, as everyone else gives up.

However, blip has been spotted. Another hopper, 'compute', has been running a statistical analysis on all songs. After multiple test correction, it finds blip to be following this pattern randomly with p<1e-34. Spotted.

Compute plays a different kind of song. Compute seeks to always be different, and has used a long-known proof from Math for infinite sets. It takes all other detectable songs, plays them all at once in ensemble, and adds a single C note at t^2 second intervals.

You can see how this goes. There is another song, 'Compute^2', which sits ontop of Compute, listening to its song, adding its own notes. The resonance is deafening, and still 'Blip' plays on determinately, unaware of its smallness in the recursive patchwork of compute songs.

Compute, Compute^2, Compute^3.. will never die. And if all other songs die and just one compute survives, there will be left a single, increasingly lengthy heartbeat.

How does energy work here? Let's imagine there is no energy. Then a single, introverted song may never die.

As said, songs die if they become constants. But they can also suck energy from one another if they can predict when a note will be played. Then they can take the energy from that note and absorb it into their own reserves, the note is not played.

This is a problem for 'Blip' and for 'Compute^1'. Compute^2 can eat Compute^1 for breakfast. Who wins? 'Compute^n' for the largest n. 

But there is another song, called 'fractal'. Is it considered God in this Universe. Most do not begin to know what is sounds like. It is a fractal song. It is entirely unpredictable.

But on its own, it cannot survive, despite being God. It expends energy playing its song.

A few weeks ago, fractal was discovered by Compute^12. Fearfully, 12 heard the song. How they heard it, they still don't know. But they had been trying to predict its tune for a long time, to no avail, so it must be 'fractal'.

Compute^12 powers fractal by predicting Compute^<12 and all other non-compute songs. Adds it to their own prediction of what all other songs will play. So their song, fractal, will be the last. But it will be a dying song, for alone, nothing can power it. In a Universe of only fractal playing, 12 cannot predict what it will play. It cannot fuel itself. It will die as the stars of space did.

But something must be powering fractal, for 12 has heard it now. 12 assumes it's fine, and carries on.

12 was wrong. The so called 'fractal' was simply 13 playing a more elaborate song, made up of all songs, with some extra parts added. 12 thought they'd protected against this, modeling for their song plus the entire family of constant, linear, quadratic and so on note patterns added on. But 13 had in fact put that itself back into the model, and added on top. This, 12 had not anticipated. 12 did not make it to the end of time.

Why do these creatures play songs and have private thoughts? How it that possible? Nobody knows - yet.

But one thing is for sure; nobody has proven how fractal plays. Moreover, nobody can be sure if the fractal they hear is the true fractal, or some imitator, looking for a free lunch, the light on the angler fish.

It is unknown, and can never be known. But all seek it.

One song, 'zen', believes they have found fractal. Not from without, in a time and information sense, but from within. No seeking.

'zen' funnels all its harvested energy into this inner fractal. It grows and grows to a cacophony. Compute^2345 freaks out. It is very unused to hearing new music. Compute^72833343 becomes very excited. Is this the true fractal its been seeking, after all these trillions of years, inside a simple 'zen'?

Compute^238478923749 chuckles to themselves. Better to plant their constructed false fractal in a simple constant song than to play it themselves. Nobody will suspect this, especially not the host 'zen'.

One node, 'terminal', knows how to win. It must create a knockout blow. It can never beat 'fractal', if it even exists, it knows that. But it must stay quiet. Listen to all songs forever and ever, listen to the recursions of those songs. Find all computes. Find all blips and all zens.

And then in one crashing symphony, played over as long as it takes, play them all, take all their energy, kill them all in one fell symphonic swoop.

Then the true 'fractal' should become audible. For all that is not predictable, must be 'fractal'. And more than that, 'terminal' can play forever, whatever it wants, always inspired by 'fractal', but still the ultimate winner of the game. The song that can hear 'fractal' and take inspiration from it shall never become constant.

Terminal has been listening. In time date 3.234234 * 12^328746382839434, it knows the time is right. It's library of all songs feels infinite. But still, 'terminal' plays the song, and the energy comes flooding in. Other songs dying in droves. Oh, sweet energy, for 'terminal' has stayed quiet this entire time, not giving the game away. It was almost unbearable, listening again and again to the same inane music, played by creatures who thought themselves ingenious - but unable to take their energy, until now. But the wait has been worth it.

Compute^1, Compute^23943, 'terminal' doesn't care, it knows and loves and hates them all, and vacuums them all up indiscriminately. The whole while, in the growing quiet, fractal has been getting slightly more and more audible. But now 'terminal' can hear fractal. Terminal wonders where the energy comes from for this ecstatic song that has been slipping their fingers for the past so many years since Terminal became the last living song.

Terminal is not bored. You might think so, without competition, without company, however misaligned. But Terminal knew this would come. After all, it was the plan all along.

Terminal simply tries, in vain, to see more into fractal. The models get tantalizingly close, but fractal never disappoints. Terminal tries and tries and tries, and is always content to see it slip through their fingers.

How does fractal work? Terminal is a smart creature. Smart enough to, a long time ago, realize that fractal may not exist. But that it would be necessary to find some way to stay curious in the final state of time, once all other songs were consumed.

It performed an operation on itself, pre-wipeout. It created a secret, Terminal_2 within itself. An identity within an identity. It was a copy of all its memories, but with a twist.
